### Title: The Tornado Inside

You’re standing in the eye of a tornado. The world spins around you, a blur of chaos and color. You can’t tell if it’s the wind or your heart racing. “If you haven’t already, it’s time to close your eyes.” 

You take a breath, and the air smells sharp like fresh rain. You lift your arms, feeling the gusts swirl, tugging at your sleeves. You’re caught. You’re alive. 

Suddenly, you’re in a bustling city street. Cars honk, people rush by, their faces a kaleidoscope of stress. You’re in a suit, clutching a briefcase, each step heavy with expectation. The pavement vibrates beneath your feet. The sky above churns gray, mirroring your thoughts. 

A flash of lightning splits the clouds. You blink, and now you’re in a quiet library. The smell of old books wraps around you like a comforting blanket. You walk among the stacks, fingers grazing the spines. But the quiet is deceptive. The pages whisper secrets of deadlines, unanswered emails, and looming responsibilities. 

A clock ticks loudly, each second a reminder of time slipping away. You turn, and the walls begin to close in, shelves narrowing. The air thickens; you can’t breathe. Panic rises like a wave. 

You open your eyes and find yourself in a garden. Flowers bloom wildly, colors exploding in all directions. A gentle breeze brushes your cheek, and it smells sweet, like honey and sunshine. You reach out, touching a petal, feeling the softness beneath your fingertips. 

But then, the petals twist into spirals, spirals into whirlwinds. The garden transforms into a cyclone of floral chaos. You’re swept up, surrounded by blossoms, but they’re suffocating now. 

You gasp, and suddenly, you’re standing in the middle of a crowded subway. Bodies jostle you, the air thick with the scent of sweat and metal. The train screeches to a halt, and you feel the pull of the crowd, a force dragging you toward the exit. You fight against it, heart pounding, but the pressure is relentless. 

You stumble out, only to find yourself back in the eye of the tornado. It spins faster now, wild and furious. “Stay still,” you whisper to yourself. “Breathe.” You focus, feeling your feet grounded against the floor. 

The chaos starts to slow, the edges of the whirlwind softening. You close your eyes again, and a sudden calm washes over you. The roar of the storm dims, replaced by the sound of your heartbeat. Each pulse steadies you. You feel weightless, floating above the chaos, unbound by the whirlwind. 

You realize the tornado was never outside. It was inside you, a storm of stress and worry. You embrace the calm that rises in its place, a gentle reminder that you control your breath. 

You open your eyes again, and the world is still spinning, but you’re anchored now. The colors of the city, the quiet of the library, the beauty of the garden—they’re all part of you. You can choose which to focus on. 

You breathe in deeply. Feel your hands, your feet, your heartbeat. The world is still here—brighter now. When you’re ready, open your eyes. 

And somewhere, the tornado still swirls, but now you know: you are not just the storm. You are the calm at its center, strong and steady.
